Attorney Technology Recommendation

The Dell 14 Plus is a strong fit for attorneys who need real performance without carrying something that feels like courtroom evidence. With a Core Ultra 7, 32 GB RAM, and a 1 TB SSD, it has enough power for research, drafting, video calls, AI tools, document chaos, and whatever “just one more tab” becomes by 2029.

Windows 11 Pro also gives the firm better security and remote management options, which means fewer support headaches and fewer laptops operating purely on vibes.

Attorney Score: 9.2 / 10. Fast, portable, future-ready, and priced low enough that finance may only sigh quietly.

Microsoft 365 License Recommendation

We evaluated three Microsoft offerings: Office 365 E5, Office 365 E3, and Microsoft 365 Business Premium.

Recommendation: Microsoft 365 Business Premium. Business Standard is great for getting work done. Business Premium is great for getting work done without accidentally helping a cybercriminal get work done too.

The upgrade adds stronger ransomware protection, better login security, device management, and tools to help keep firm laptops, phones, and client data under control. Think of it as Business Standard plus a security guard who never sleeps and is deeply suspicious of every email attachment.

Cost Summary:
Current Microsoft 365 Business Standard cost: 9 users × $15/month = $135/month.
Microsoft 365 Business Premium: 9 users × $26.40/month = $237.60/month.
Difference: +$102.60/month, or +$1,231.20/year.

For a security-minded law firm, that works out to roughly the cost of a few billable hours per year in exchange for significantly stronger protection against ransomware, account compromise, and device-related security issues.
