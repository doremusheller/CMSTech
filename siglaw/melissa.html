Expanded Biography

Justice through service.

Melissa's legal career has been defined by a commitment to justice. Early in her practice, she focused extensively on criminal prosecution, serving in Special Victims Units and working to combat child sex trafficking both domestically and internationally.

In Hawaii, she served as an Analyst and Special Prosecutor for the Hawaii Internet Crimes Against Children Unit within the Hawaii Attorney General's Office. She also served as a Legislative Attorney for the Judiciary Chair at the Hawaii State Legislature, helping shape policy affecting families, children, and communities.

Beyond courtroom advocacy, Melissa has served as a law professor at the University of Arizona's Rogers College of Law, Faulkner University's Jones School of Law, and the University of Hawaii's Richardson School of Law, mentoring future attorneys and legal leaders.

Today, Melissa serves as a Founding Partner at Signature Law Partners, PLLC, where she continues to advocate for women and children, assist entrepreneurs in building successful businesses, and zealously litigate on behalf of her clients. Operating through a boutique cloud-based law firm model, she provides clients with flexible and effective representation regardless of geography.

Melissa remains deeply committed to public service. She served on Hawaii's Joint Venture Education Forum for three years and on Ho'Ola Na Pua's Executive Board of Directors for six years. She currently serves as Chair of Washington D.C.'s CGO Foundation and on the Common Sense Media Advisory Board.

Her work combating child sex trafficking and helping advance Arizona's Purple Star School legislation has been featured in numerous publications. As a USAF senior leader spouse, Melissa is also a sought-after speaker at MAJCOM command conferences, national women's symposiums, and international podcasts.
