Expanded Bio

A reputation that travels by current.

Don began his career in harbor disputes, salvage claims, and aggressively worded cease-and-desist letters printed on waterproof stationery. He quickly became known for identifying weak arguments, wrapping them tightly, and not releasing them until opposing counsel reconsidered their entire career.

His litigation style is direct, dramatic, and legally suffocating in precisely the way malpractice carriers prefer not to discuss. Clients rely on Don when the matter involves corporate conflict, regulatory turbulence, maritime exposure, or an opponent foolish enough to underestimate mollusks.

At any given moment, Don may be drafting a motion, reviewing exhibits, marking deposition transcripts, intimidating expert witnesses, and opening a jar of olives without assistance.
